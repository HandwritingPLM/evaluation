Uses and Gratifications

The uses and gratifications approach emphasizes motives and behaviors. Blumler and Katz (1974) concluded that different individuals have unique needs and purposes. The same media content may gratify different needs. There is not only one way that people use media. Contrarily, there are as many ways of using the media as there are media users (Blumler & Katz 1974). Basic needs, social situation, and the individual's background, such as education, affect people's ideas about what they want from media.
