Like many other structuralist scholars, Barthes was influenced by Saussure's structural linguistics. 

To Saussure, the linguistic mechanism operates on two levels, the system: langue and the performance: parole. Langue is the characteristics and set of conventions necessary for communication, indifferent to the material of the signals which compose it, as opposed to parole which covers the purely individual part of language (Barthes, 1977, p. 13). Barthes interprets Saussure's linguistic system within the social dimension. The abstract level, langue, is the social convention of values shared throughout a society which is established and adapted by practice. The concrete, parol, is the actual expression of the individual voice.
