Therefore, uncertainty is a concept that plays a significant role in communication. The following theorists explore how communication can be a vehicle individuals utilize to navigate uncertainty.

Early Influences

The following theorists significantly contributed to the examination of uncertainty in communication. The initial research of uncertainty was reflected in assumptions of Berger (n.d.) and Calabrese (n.d.). Berger, who studied at the University of Iowa under the direction of Kurt Lewin, Lewin being one of the founders of social psychology and a pioneer in the research on group dynamics, had a substantial influence on understanding uncertainty in interpersonal communication. Calabrese initially worked at the University of Rochester, but in 1945 he followed Lewin to the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, where the Research Center for Group Dynamics was located.

After graduation, Turman (2000) initially followed in the footsteps of his mentors, whose research was highly influential. Turman recognized the importance of understanding uncertainty and its impact on individuals coming from different backgrounds. He acknowledged the significant influence he received from his mentors, stating, "It was in this environment that I learned..."
