Chapter 4

Uses and Gratifications

1.1 Introduction

Where and how people approach the use of media has always been a matter of interest in the field of Communications Studies. The origin of this area of research lies in the desire to explain the great appeal of certain media to individuals. The question of how people choose and consume media has been a topic of great interest and has led to various theories and approaches. This chapter explores the concept of uses and gratifications, focusing on the different ways people engage with media and the reasons behind their choices.

1.2 Origin and History

It is important to understand that communication theories have developed considerably over the past 100 years. During this time, researchers have sought to understand the relationship between people and media, exploring the various functions and effects of media consumption.
