Like many of his predecessors, Harold Lasswell was a key figure in the development of propaganda theory. He believed that propaganda played a central role in shaping public opinion and, consequently, the functioning of democracy. This article aims to highlight Lasswell's contribution to the field of propaganda and the impact of his work. Lasswell argued that propaganda was a form of communication that aimed to influence people's attitudes and behaviors in a particular direction. He believed that propaganda was not limited to governments or political entities but was also used by various organizations, such as businesses and media outlets. Lasswell's work laid the foundation for the study of propaganda and its effects on society.
